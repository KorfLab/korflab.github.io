KorfLab

Welcome to the home page of Ian Korf’s research group at UC Davis. Our lab uses computational methods to study problems in molecular biology. More specifically, we are interested in the mappings from structure to function. We are particularly interested in discerning biological signal from biological noise. Our favorite topics include gene prediction, intron function, gene expression, sequence alignment, protein-DNA interactions, motif-finding, NMR analysis, genome assembly, repeat analysis, and almost anything that mixes genotype and phenotype. We love developing new methods in emerging disciplines but also love the classics.
